Character Generation

Characters are represented by a set of attributes that relate to the innate ability to perform a set of actions. Attributes are represented as numbers between 0 and 100. An attribute score of 10 is considered to be about average. The following chart gives an indication of what attribute values mean.
ValueDescriptor
00-05Poor
06-09Below Average
10-13Average
14-17Above Average
18-21Excellent
22-25Exceptional
26-29Superior
30-33Phenomenal
34+Legendary
The attributes are grouped according to physical, social, and mental abilities. Physical attributes are strength, agility, and endurance. Social attributes are perception, dexterity, and charisma. Mental attributes are intelligence, wisdom, and concentration. A character also has two derived attributes, which are health and spirit. Health is derived from the physical attributes of endurance and strength (HP = (2*END+STR)/3), while spirit is derived from the mental attributes of focus and wisdom (SP = (2*FOC+WIS)/3). As a character sustains physical damage, health is reduced. If health is completely depleted, the character is incapacitated. As a character utilizes skills, spirit is reduced. If spirit is completely depleted, a character cannot perform any skill-dependent actions.
Characters are created by distributing 100 attribute points across the 9 attributes. Therefore, if each attribute was set at 11 (with one at 12), the resulting character would be entirely balanced, but completely average. In this case, the chance of success for this character of completing an action of average complexity would be 36%. Investing one skill point in the skill associated with the action would improve the chance of success to 62%.
